In July 2024, they decided to take control of their energy costs by installing a 10.35 kW solar system through a local installer. With Alberta's Net Billing program, every extra kilowatt-hour their panels generated in the summer rolled forward as credits toward their winter bills, balancing out seasonal usage.

Solar Panels in Snow?

A common myth is that solar panels aren't economical in Alberta due to snow and winter. This is false thanks to net billing and long summer days.

A photo of the Edmonton solar panel system in winter. The system was installed on a south-facing roof, and the steep pitch helps snow slide off within a day or two of snowfall. Meanwhile, summer production is so strong that credits carry through the entire winter.

Their Electric Bill with Solar

The June 2025 bill shows how solar dramatically offsets electricity costs. While the homeowner used 234 kWh, their solar system exported far more to the grid. Because solar overproduction rolls forward or gets refunded, these monthly credits accelerate the system's ROI timeline, helping the homeowner reach break-even years sooner.

This will result in over 8,000 in lifetime electricity savings as the solar system will produce optimal output for 25-30 years.
