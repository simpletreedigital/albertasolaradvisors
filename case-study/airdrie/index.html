Their Electric Bill with Solar

This bill shows how solar wipes out most charges. From Jun 2-19, the home used 269 kWh. Charges were Energy 0.70, Transmission and Distribution 8.51, plus Municipal .78 and Retailer .85, totaling 33.84.

Because the system exported more than it imported, the Microgen credit was -83.25, which more than offset those costs. After GST -.47, the statement ended at Total Due -1.88, a credit that rolls forward to future bills.

These recurring summer credits help cover higher winter usage, accelerate ROI, and bring break-even sooner, compounding to 0k+ in lifetime savings over 25-30 years.

Three Levers That Maximized ROI

Net Billing (Micro-Generation)

Alberta's net-billing framework lets the family earn credits for the extra energy their system exports, especially in those long, bright summer months. Those credits then help offset winter bills when the sun is lower. Net billing is the backbone of the ROI, letting summer do the heavy lifting for the year.

Alberta Solar Club (Optimized Retail Rates)

The family enrolled with an electricity retailer offering an Alberta Solar Club-style program, which tweaks buy/sell rates to better match solar-home realities. Better export terms in high-production months and smart import pricing when they need to pull from the grid. This optimization smooths cash flow and can shave months (sometimes years) off payback.

Alberta CEIP Loan (No Upfront Cost)

The Daws chose CEIP financing, putting the project on the property tax bill at a competitive rate, spread over a comfortable term. It's tied to the property, not the people. If they ever sell, the remaining obligation and energy savings can transfer to the next owner. For the Daws, CEIP meant /bin/sh upfront, with most of the payment offset by the bill reduction from day one.

The Day-to-Day Experience

The family wanted simplicity: set it, forget it, and check in when curious. Their system came with a mobile monitoring app that shows real-time and historical production. They can see exactly how many kWh their roof harvested today, last week, and month-to-date, plus how that stacks up against their typical use. It's oddly addictive, and it turns the kids into mini energy detectives.
