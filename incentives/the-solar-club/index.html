What is The Solar Club™?

The Solar Club™ is a special electricity rate plan for Alberta solar homeowners that allows you to switch between two electricity rates based on the season. Both the import and export sides of your bill use the same selected rate during a billing cycle.

Available through select Alberta electricity retailers, it's designed to maximize the value of your solar exports during peak production months and minimize your import costs during low-production winter months.

You can toggle between rates by submitting a quick request through your Solar Club™ retailer's website. Most homeowners switch to the high rate around March or April and drop back to the low rate in October or November, aligning with solar production cycles.

Who Should Join the Solar Club™?

The Solar Club™ works best for homes that generate 70% or more of their annual electricity needs from solar. These homes typically export surplus electricity in summer, allowing them to build valuable credits at the high $0.30/kWh rate.

If your system offsets less than 70% of your usage, you may not export enough to benefit from the high rate , a standard fixed rate might make more sense.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Be a micro-generator under 150 kW
  • Use a bi-directional cumulative meter (not an interval meter)
  • Not in Medicine Hat, Peigan REA, Ermineskin REA, Wild Rose REA, Lakeland REA, North Parkland Power REA, or Blue Mountain Power Co-op
  • A charitable donation to your local food bank is encouraged

When Should I Switch Rates?

The general rule is simple:

  • If your home is exporting more electricity than it's using → switch to the high rate ($0.30/kWh)
  • If you're consuming more than you're producing → switch to the low rate ($0.0877/kWh)

Most Alberta homes with well-sized solar setups hit net-export status by spring and revert by fall. The program allows rate switching with just 10 days' notice, no penalties. Your retailer may even send email reminders in spring and fall.

Pre-Solar Rate

You can pre-enroll in the Solar Club™ as soon as you've signed a solar installation contract , even before your panels are turned on. During the waiting period (up to 180 days), you'll be placed on a pre-solar rate of $0.065/kWh.

Are Rates the Same Across Retailers?

Yes , all participating Solar Club™ retailers offer the same high and low energy rates. However, monthly admin fees vary, so it's worth comparing retailers. Some offer signup incentives , contact us for the latest referral codes.

Why Choose the Solar Club™?

Here's a simple example to show the power of the Solar Club™:

  • Your home uses 8,000 kWh per year
  • Your solar system produces 8,000 kWh per year
  • In summer, you export 2,000 kWh → credit built at $0.30/kWh = $600 credit
  • In winter, you import 2,000 kWh at $0.0877/kWh = $175.40 cost
  • Net result: $600 credit more than covers $175 winter import + fixed fees

The difference in rates, combined with rollover credit, boosts your solar ROI by 3–4 years and flattens seasonal billing swings.

Can I Get $0 Power Bills?

You absolutely can , if your solar system covers 100% or more of your annual energy needs. Here's how:

  • In summer, export excess energy at the high rate and build credit
  • That credit can be applied toward everything on your electricity bill, including fixed fees
  • Extra credits roll over month-to-month

Many Alberta homes report $0 bills from May through September , and some see it stretch into the fall.

What About All Those "Other Fees"?

In Alberta, fees like transmission, distribution, and local access charges are mostly variable , tied to how much electricity you use. When your solar production offsets your usage, you reduce these fees too. And when you export electricity at the Solar Club™ high rate, you build credits that can be applied to cover these charges.

Yes , solar helps reduce "other fees" too. The Solar Club™ high-rate credits can effectively wipe out your entire electricity bill, including the line items most people think are unavoidable.

Who Funds the High Rate?

The Solar Club™ high rate is made possible through the Alberta Electric System Operator (AESO), which helps balance grid supply. The rate is determined by each Solar Club™ retailer under Alberta's Microgeneration Regulation. One key rule: the buy and sell rate must match during a billing cycle.

Will the Solar Club™ Always Be Available?

The Microgeneration Regulation could change in the future, and there's no guarantee the Solar Club™ will remain unchanged. That said, there are no known plans to alter or phase out the program. Installing sooner ensures your interconnection is established under current terms.
