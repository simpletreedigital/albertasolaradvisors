Calgary Solar: The Financial Case

Calgary sits at about 1,048 metres elevation and 51 degrees north latitude, positioning it among Canada's sunniest major cities. A south-facing rooftop system here typically produces 1,300 to 1,350 kWh per installed kilowatt annually, slightly more than Edmonton. Combined with ENMAX's net billing framework and the optional Solar Club rate of $0.30/kWh for summer exports, the economics are strong.

Calgary homeowners have three electricity retailers to consider: ENMAX Energy, direct energy, and various retail electricity providers. The Solar Club rate is exclusive to ENMAX customers. For those on other retailers, standard net billing under the provincial Micro-Generation Regulation applies, valued at your current retail rate.

CEIP Status for Calgary

Calgary's Clean Energy Improvement Program is currently paused pending a new funding intake. The program has run several successful rounds in recent years, and we expect it to reopen. Homeowners who want to act now can explore bridge loan financing at rates starting around 6.99%, with the option to refinance through CEIP when it reopens.

We maintain a waiting list for Calgary CEIP. Homeowners who register now get their proposal prepared in advance so they can apply immediately when the program reopens. Visit our CEIP page for current status across all Alberta municipalities.

Local Case Study

Elbow Park Calgary: 10 kW System, Solar Club Enrollment

The Situation

A 2,600 sq ft Calgary home in Elbow Park with ENMAX service and a large south and west-facing roof. Pre-solar ENMAX bills averaged $2,800 per year. The homeowner was interested in maximizing summer export value.

Our Approach

We designed a 10 kW system and modeled both standard net billing and Solar Club enrollment against the homeowner's monthly consumption profile. Solar Club's $0.30/kWh summer rate provided better annual economics given the system's large summer surplus.

The Outcome

After Solar Club enrollment, annual electricity savings reached $2,600. The net billing credit bank under Solar Club doesn't roll forward, but the premium summer rate more than compensated. Total electricity costs dropped from $2,800 to under $200 per year.
