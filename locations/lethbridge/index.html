Solar in Lethbridge: Among the Best in Canada

Lethbridge benefits from some of the highest solar irradiance in the country. Located in southern Alberta at roughly 49 degrees north latitude , similar to the northern United States , the city averages over 2,500 hours of sunshine annually. A south-facing rooftop here produces approximately 1,350 to 1,420 kWh per installed kilowatt per year, making system economics particularly strong.

The combination of high production and Alberta's retail-rate net billing means a typical 8 kW system offsets $2,000 to $2,300 in annual electricity costs. Lethbridge's CEIP program is currently paused but has historically opened for new applicants when provincial funding is renewed. Contact us to get on our notification list.

Installation and Permitting in Lethbridge

Residential solar in Lethbridge requires a building permit from the City of Lethbridge Development Services. FortisAlberta is the distribution utility for the area and handles interconnection applications. One factor unique to Lethbridge is the city's famous Chinook winds , installers in the area use wind-rated mounting hardware appropriate for the local environment, and our site assessments account for this.

The city's sunny, dry climate also means minimal snow load concerns on most installations , Lethbridge's Chinooks routinely clear snow from panels within hours of any snowfall, maintaining strong winter production that many Alberta cities can't match.

About Lethbridge

Lethbridge is southern Alberta's largest city, situated on the Oldman River at the edge of the prairies and close to the Montana border. Founded in 1882 as a coal mining centre, it evolved into an agricultural hub and university city. Today, with around 100,000 residents, it's known for its exceptionally sunny climate, the striking High Level Bridge (one of the world's longest railway trestle bridges), and the University of Lethbridge.

The city's climate , dry, sunny, and moderated by frequent Chinook winds , makes it one of the best solar markets in the country. For building permits, visit City of Lethbridge Building Permits. For interconnection details, see FortisAlberta's micro-generation portal.
