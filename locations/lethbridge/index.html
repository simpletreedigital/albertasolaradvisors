Why Lethbridge Is a Strong Solar Market

Lethbridge sits at 49.7 degrees north, the southernmost of Alberta's major cities, and benefits from significantly more annual sunshine than Edmonton or Red Deer. The city averages over 2,400 hours of sunshine per year, among the highest in Canada. Chinook winds keep the climate milder than the rest of the province in winter, which also reduces snow accumulation on panels and extends the shoulder season for solar production.

A south-facing system in Lethbridge typically produces 1,350 to 1,400 kWh per installed kilowatt per year. An 8 kW system here can produce over 11,000 kWh annually, offsetting the majority of a typical 14,000 to 16,000 kWh household consumption. With FortisAlberta net billing and Lethbridge's active CEIP program, the financial case is among the strongest of any Alberta city.

Lethbridge CEIP Program Details

Lethbridge's Clean Energy Improvement Program is currently open for new applications. The program offers financing at 3.75% over 5 to 25 years, repaid through the municipal property tax bill. The CEIP loan attaches to the property and transfers to a buyer on sale.

Applications require a qualified solar proposal. Our team prepares full CEIP-ready documentation for Lethbridge homeowners. See complete program details and current municipality status on our CEIP overview page.

Local Case Study

Lethbridge Westside Home: Near Net-Zero on CEIP Financing

The Situation

A homeowner in Lethbridge's west end (near Country Club Road) with a 2,400 sq ft home and an annual electricity spend of $2,600 on FortisAlberta. Excellent south and southwest roof exposure with no significant shading.

Our Approach

We designed a 9.6 kW system producing approximately 13,400 kWh per year. The additional production relative to Edmonton peers reflects Lethbridge's higher irradiance. CEIP financing covered the full $28,000 installation at 3.75% over 20 years.

The Outcome

Annual electricity savings: $2,400. Annual CEIP payment: $1,980. Net monthly improvement: $35 from year one. Electricity costs reduced from $2,600 to approximately $200 per year in fixed charges.
