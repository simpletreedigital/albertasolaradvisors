Installation and Permitting in Airdrie

Solar installations in Airdrie require a development permit through the City of Airdrie's Planning Services, plus a separate building permit. The city has a dedicated process for solar panel permits, with typical approval times of 2 to 3 weeks. FortisAlberta handles interconnection for Airdrie properties, typically adding another 3 to 5 weeks to the timeline after installation and inspection.

Many Airdrie homes are in newer subdivisions with HOA or architectural guidelines , we review any applicable restrictions as part of our site assessment to avoid surprises during permitting.

About Airdrie

Airdrie is one of Canada's fastest-growing cities, located just 30 kilometres north of Calgary along the Queen Elizabeth II Highway. Originally a small railway town, it has grown dramatically over the past three decades as Calgary's suburban footprint expanded northward. With a population now exceeding 80,000, Airdrie boasts a high proportion of newer housing stock , which translates directly into good solar installation conditions, with modern roof structures and unobstructed sun exposure in many subdivisions.

The city is served by FortisAlberta for electricity distribution. For permitting information, visit City of Airdrie Development Permits. FortisAlberta's micro-generation portal covers the interconnection process for Airdrie properties.
