Why Medicine Hat Is One of Alberta's Best Solar Cities

With roughly 330+ sunny days per year, Medicine Hat sits at the top of Alberta's solar resource map. That translates directly into higher annual production from any given system size. An 8 kW system here commonly produces 10,800 kWh or more annually, about 6% above the Edmonton-area average, which compounds meaningfully over a 25-year system life.

The city's CEIP program is currently open, offering 3.75% financing through the municipal property tax roll. Electricity distribution in most of the city is handled by Medicine Hat's own municipal utility, which manages net billing locally. Export credits are applied at retail rate, and the favorable sun hours mean more of your production turns into banked credits during peak summer months.

Medicine Hat CEIP Application

Medicine Hat's CEIP is administered through the ceip.abmunis.ca portal. Applications require a qualified solar proposal from an approved installer. Our team prepares the documentation and manages the application from submission through approval. Typical processing time is 2 to 4 weeks.

Building permits for solar are issued by the City of Medicine Hat Planning and Development Services. Our team handles all permit preparation and submission as part of the installation process.

Local Case Study

Medicine Hat: 9.2 kW, CEIP-Financed, Highest-Production Year in Client Portfolio

The Situation

A homeowner in Medicine Hat, 2,600 sq ft home, south-facing rear roof with no shading. Pre-solar electricity spend: $2,700 per year. Municipal utility service. CEIP open at time of installation.

Our Approach

9.2 kW system, 23 x 400W panels. CEIP at 3.75% over 20 years, monthly tax addition of $167. Projected annual production: 11,500 kWh, saving $2,070 in electricity costs. Net positive from day one.

The Outcome

Actual year-one production came in at 11,720 kWh, above projection. Annual electricity savings: $2,109. Net benefit after CEIP payments: $105 in year one, growing as electricity rates rise. Break-even projected at year 11.
